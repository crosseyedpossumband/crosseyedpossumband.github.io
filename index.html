The Cross-Eyed Possum

Winners of the 2022 Pickin in the Pines Bluegrass Music Festival

Experience the vibrant sound of The Cross-Eyed Possum, a dynamic Newgrass/Americana ensemble sweeping the nation with their mesmerizing performances. As identical twins at the helm, they infuse every festival and event with a unique blend of musical artistry!

From Pickin’ in The Pines to the historic 40th Annual Huck Finn Jubilee, their signature vocal harmonies echo the legendary Crosby Stills and Nash, with technical mastery that rivals that of The Punch Brothers. This band provides an auditory experience that is both profound and exhilarating. With original compositions drawing inspiration from these iconic groups, enriched by deep-rooted passion for jazz and classical music.

Join us in celebrating the latest creative milestone, "Out On My Own," the second studio album launched in July 2023.

Jason Howard

Upright Bass, Vocals
Jason Howard is a distinguished double bass virtuoso whose musical journey began in middle school and has flourished into a professional career with the acclaimed Cross-Eyed Possum. Upon completing his music performance degree from Northern Arizona State University in May 2022, Jason has continued to showcase his remarkable talent alongside his twin brother, Jonah.

Throughout his high school tenure, Jason achieved remarkable success in a variety of competitive arenas, excelling in both orchestra and jazz competitions. He was a two-time All-State musician, clinching first place in orchestra once and securing the top spot in jazz on two occasions. These early achievements laid the foundation for his blossoming career, which was further enriched by his collaborative performances with his twin brother, captivating audiences across Arizona.

Jason's passion for bluegrass and jazz led him to join "Sugar and the Mint," a band with which he won numerous accolades, including the prestigious Telluride Bluegrass Festival Band Contest. This experience not only honed his competitive edge but also allowed him to extensively tour the music festival circuit, deepening his exposure to diverse musical styles.

His tenure at Northern Arizona University was marked by significant achievements, including serving as the principal bassist for the NAU Symphony, Flagstaff Symphony, Verde Valley Symphony, and performing with the university's top jazz combo. Jason's exceptional talent was recognized when he won the NAU concerto competition in his junior year, a testament to his mastery and creativity.

As a member of The Cross-Eyed Possum, Jason has toured the nation, sharing the stage with his brother and contributing to the band's unique sound through his original double bass compositions. His profound musical background, combined with his dedication to both performance and composition, underscores Jason Howard's position as a formidable talent in the contemporary music scene.

Jonah Howard

Guitar, Vocals
Jonah Howard, an accomplished musician and a summa cum laude graduate from Arizona State University, earned his degree in Jazz Performance and a certificate in Music Entrepreneurship in May 2022. Throughout his collegiate career, Jonah's musical journey was enriched by multiple tours with the celebrated Cross-Eyed Possum, as well as performances with various bands across the vibrant Phoenix music scene.

Under the esteemed guidance of Michael Kocour and Lewis Nash at ASU, Jonah honed his craft within some of the university's premier ensembles, showcasing his versatility and commitment to musical excellence. His creative spirit flourished during this time, leading to the production of numerous original jazz compositions for recitals and coursework within ASU's Popular Music School. This repertoire of innovative music is set to enhance future Cross-Eyed Possum projects and has already found its way into collaborative efforts, such as the "Power Play" event, a partnership between ASU and Phoenix Children's Hospital.

Jonah's musical prowess was evident early on, as demonstrated by his remarkable achievement of being selected for the Arizona All State Jazz Band for four consecutive years (2015-2018) during his time at Prescott High School. This period also saw him being named Jazz Student of the Year at the Prescott Jazz Summit festival in 2015, a testament to his exceptional talent and dedication to jazz.

Even before embarking on his professional career, Jonah displayed an eagerness to explore the breadth of his musical abilities, participating in every available band opportunity and mastering a diverse array of instruments, including the cello, French horn, guitar, mellophone, and mandolin. This early exposure to a wide range of musical experiences has undoubtedly contributed to his dynamic approach to performance and composition, making Jonah Howard a multifaceted talent within The Cross-Eyed Possum and the broader music community.

Keenan Hammack

Mandolin, Vocals
Keenan Hammack, a prodigious talent in the acoustic music scene, has swiftly ascended to prominence through his remarkable achievements and musical contributions. His journey began with a triumph at the prestigious Telluride Bluegrass Band Contest in 2017, a victory that marked him as a force to be reckoned with in the bluegrass community. This accomplishment was followed by another notable success when he and The Cross-Eyed Possum clinched the title at the esteemed 2022 Pickin' in the Pines band contest.

At the young age of 22, Keenan's musical résumé is already impressive, boasting multiple national tours with diverse projects and a series of professionally produced studio albums. His unique creative energy, which he describes as quasi-chaotic, defines him as a natural performer and entertainer with the rare ability to engage audiences in a deeply human, thought-provoking musical journey.

Keenan's musical expertise spans a wide array of genres, including pop, rock, classical, jazz, country, swing, Americana, and bluegrass. Drawing on this eclectic mix of influences, he crafts original music that transcends traditional boundaries, offering listeners a rich, immersive experience. He believes that music, much like a favorite book, should transport its audience on an emotional and intellectual journey, prompting reflection and evoking deep feelings.

Through his innovative approach and multifaceted talent, Keenan Hammack stands as a beacon of creativity in the contemporary music landscape, inviting listeners to explore the depths of their own experiences through his meticulously curated musical adventures.

A Fresh Take on Tradition: An In-Depth Review of Out On My Own

In the world of bluegrass, folk, and blues, it's a rare gem to stumble upon an album that manages to honor its roots while simultaneously breaking the mold. "Out On My Own" by The Cross-Eyed Possum is one such treasure, a musical odyssey that captivates from the first chord to the last. This review dives into the heart of what makes this album a journey through the landscapes of engaging bluegrass, emotive folk, and soul-stirring blues.

At the core of "Out On My Own" is a trio whose harmonies are nothing short of perfect. Their ability to weave their voices together with such precision and emotion is a testament to their chemistry and artistry. The harmonies are not just well-placed; they are masterfully executed, enhancing the storytelling in each track and enveloping the listener in a warm, sonic embrace.

The upright bass work is, in a word, masterful. Before you're even aware it's happened, the bassist transitions to playing with a bow, a moment that is as surprising as it is mesmerizing. It's moments like these that underscore the album's innovation, taking what could have been a traditional bluegrass or folk experience and elevating it to something entirely otherworldly.

Equally commendable is the guitar work and the mandolin playing, both of which are executed with precision and flair. The guitar provides a sturdy backbone for the melodies to dance upon, while the mandolin sprinkles its own unique sparkle across the tracks. Together, they create a rich, layered sound that is both inviting and invigorating.

What sets "Out On My Own" apart is its palpable sense of joy and enthusiasm. This is an upbeat, fun album that doesn't just aim to entertain but also to connect with its listeners on a deeper level. It's evident that The Cross-Eyed Possum pours their heart and soul into their music, and this emotional investment pays off in spades, creating an album that is as emotionally resonant as it is technically impressive.

"Out On My Own" by The Cross-Eyed Possum is a standout sophmore album that deserves a spot in the music collection of anyone who appreciates the artistry of bluegrass, folk, and blues. It's a refreshing take on traditional genres, brimming with emotion, technical prowess, and a clear love for music-making. Whether you're a longtime fan of these styles or a newcomer looking to explore, "Out On My Own" is an exhilarating musical adventure that promises to leave a lasting impression.
